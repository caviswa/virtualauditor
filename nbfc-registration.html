NBFC Registration

NBFC (Non-Banking Financial Company) registration with the Reserve Bank of India is mandatory for any company engaged in the business of loans, investments, or acquisition of shares/securities. Minimum NOF (Net Owned Funds): ₹10 crore for new NBFCs (increased from ₹2 crore). When Is NBFC Registration Not Required?

NBFC registration may not be required when: (a) the company's principal business is not financial activity (financial assets less than 50% of total assets and financial income less than 50% of gross income), (b) the lending activity qualifies under exempted categories (e.g., certain housing finance, insurance, stock broking), (c) the company is a government company or statutory corporation, or (d) the Net Owned Fund is below Rs 10 Cr (minimum requirement). Operating as an NBFC without RBI registration is a criminal offence under Section 45-IA of the RBI Act.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum capital for NBFC?

Minimum Net Owned Funds: ₹10 crore for new NBFC registrations (as per RBI revised norms). Existing NBFCs have transitional compliance timelines.

How long does NBFC registration take?

6-12 months. RBI conducts detailed due diligence on promoters, business plan, capital adequacy, and compliance framework before granting registration.

What is NBFC and who needs registration?

Non-Banking Financial Company — company engaged in lending, investment, insurance, chit fund, etc. Registration with RBI mandatory if principal business is financial activity. Net Owned Funds minimum: ₹2 crore.

What are the types of NBFC?

NBFC-ICC (Investment and Credit), NBFC-MFI (Microfinance), NBFC-Factor, NBFC-P2P (Peer to Peer), NBFC-AA (Account Aggregator), and Housing Finance Company. Each has specific regulations and NOF requirements.

Can an existing company convert to NBFC?

Yes, if it meets NOF requirement and has no disqualifying activities. Must cease prohibited activities before applying. Directors must meet RBI fit and proper criteria.

What compliance is needed after NBFC registration?

Monthly NBS-ALM returns, quarterly CRAR computation, annual statutory audit, RBI Master Direction compliance, KYC/AML policies, grievance redressal mechanism, and annual fair practices code compliance.
