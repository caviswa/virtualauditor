3. Our Company Secretary Services Services in Hebbal

Company secretary services are the governance backbone of every registered company — the regulatory infrastructure that keeps the company compliant with the Companies Act 2013, Secretarial Standards, and (for companies with FDI) FEMA reporting requirements. For businesses in Hebbal, Bangalore, the compliance obligations are identical to any other location — but the quality of secretarial support determines whether the company passes investor due diligence, avoids director disqualification, and maintains its good standing with the ROC.

The most dangerous secretarial failure is not a missed filing — it is the accumulation of small gaps over 2-3 years that collectively create a compliance crisis. No AOC-4 for 2 years → ₹73,000+ in late fees per form. No DIR-3 KYC → DIN deactivated, director cannot sign any document. No AGM for 2+ years → ROC initiates strike-off proceedings under Section 248. No MGT-7A for 3 years → directors disqualified under Section 164(2) from ALL company boards for 5 years. Each gap is individually manageable when caught early. Together, they cost ₹5-10 lakh in remediation and create existential risk.

Virtual Auditor's secretarial practice is led by an ACS (Associate Company Secretary, ICSI) combined with FCA — a rare dual qualification that eliminates the coordination gap between separate CS and CA firms. When a share allotment needs a board resolution (CS function), a valuation report (CA function), and an FC-GPR filing (FEMA function) — one firm handles all three. This integrated approach ensures the board resolution language aligns with FEMA requirements, the ROC filing timeline matches the FEMA filing timeline, and no gap exists between corporate and regulatory compliance.

For startups in Hebbal, Bangalore preparing for fundraise, the secretarial compliance history is one of the first things investor DD teams examine. A clean minutes book with properly drafted resolutions, up-to-date statutory registers reconciled with MCA filings, and timely annual returns signal professional governance. Gaps in any of these areas trigger DD findings that translate to valuation adjustments, escrow demands, or specific indemnity requirements. The annual retainer of ₹3,000-₹12,500/month is always cheaper than the ₹2-5 lakh remediation that becomes necessary when governance is neglected.

Our secretarial services cover the complete lifecycle: board meeting management (agenda, resolutions, minutes, SS-1 compliance), annual ROC filings (AOC-4, MGT-7A, ADT-1, DIR-3 KYC), event-based filings (PAS-3, DIR-12, SH-7, CHG-1), statutory register maintenance, ESOP administration (scheme design through exercise processing), related party transaction compliance (Section 188 approvals, Form AOC-2), secretarial audit (Form MR-3 for applicable companies), and corporate governance advisory (board composition, committee structure, vigil mechanism). Every client receives a customized compliance calendar with automated reminders — no deadline missed since inception of the practice.

StepActionDetails
1Compliance AssessmentReview your company's current secretarial compliance status — pending ROC filings, missing board resolutions, statutory register gaps, and AGM/meeting compliance. Identify RED (urgent), AMBER (fix within quarter), and GREEN (compliant) items.
2Board Meeting Setup and ManagementSchedule and manage board meetings (minimum 4/year, gap ≤120 days). Prepare agenda, circulate notice (7 days), ensure quorum, draft resolutions with FEMA/regulatory language where applicable, and record minutes within 15 days.
3Annual ROC FilingsFile AOC-4 (financial statements, 30 days from AGM), MGT-7A (annual return, 60 days from AGM), ADT-1 (auditor appointment), DIR-3 KYC (all directors, September 30). Late filing: ₹100/day per form with no cap.
4Statutory Register MaintenanceMaintain and update: Register of Members (within 7 days of every allotment/transfer), Register of Directors and KMP, Register of Charges, Register of Contracts with Related Parties (Section 189), and Minutes Books (board + general meetings).
5Event-Based FilingsFile within prescribed timelines: PAS-3 (share allotment, 15 days), DIR-12 (director appointment/resignation, 30 days), SH-7 (capital alteration, 30 days), CHG-1 (charge creation, 30 days), MGT-14 (special resolutions, 30 days).
6Governance Advisory and Compliance CalendarMaintain a client-specific compliance calendar with automated reminders 30, 15, and 7 days before each deadline. Advise on board composition, committee structure, independent director requirements, vigil mechanism, and Section 188 RPT compliance.
