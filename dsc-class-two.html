Class 2 Digital Signature Certificate — Uses and Process

Class 2 DSC provides medium level of assurance about the identity of the signer. It is the most commonly used DSC for business filings in India, accepted by MCA, Income Tax, GST, EPFO, and most government portals.

Verification process: Class 2 DSC requires video-based KYC (no in-person visit). Applicant provides PAN, Aadhaar, passport photo, and completes video verification with the Certifying Authority. Token (USB device) delivered within 1-3 working days.

Important notes: DSC is issued to an individual (not a company). Directors use their personal DSC for company filings. One DSC can be used across multiple companies. Validity: 1-2 years (choose at purchase). Cannot be renewed — a new certificate is issued before expiry.

When Is Class 2 Digital Signature Certificate Not Required?

Class 2 DSC has been discontinued by CCA (Controller of Certifying Authorities). All new applications are processed as Class 3. If you have an existing Class 2 DSC that is still valid, it will work until expiry. For new requirements, a Class 3 DSC will be issued.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who needs Class 2 DSC?

All company directors, LLP designated partners, and professionals filing returns on behalf of clients.

What is Class 2 DSC used for?

MCA company filings, Income Tax e-filing, GST registration and returns, EPFO filings. Accepted by all government portals except e-tendering.

How much does Class 2 DSC cost?

Rs 800-1,500 for 2-year validity. Issued within 1-3 working days with video KYC. Requires PAN and Aadhaar.

Can one DSC be used for multiple companies?

Yes. DSC is issued to an individual. Directors use their personal DSC across all companies where they hold directorship.

What is the validity of Class 2 DSC?

1-2 years chosen at purchase. Cannot be renewed - new certificate issued before expiry on same or new USB token.

How to use DSC for MCA filing?

Install emSigner utility, register DSC on MCA portal under your DIN, attach while filing forms. System validates against registered DSC.
