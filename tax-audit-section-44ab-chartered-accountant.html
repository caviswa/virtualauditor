Frequently Asked Questions

Who needs tax audit under Section 44AB?

Businesses with turnover exceeding Rs 1 crore (Rs 10 crores if cash receipts and payments are within 5% of total). Professionals with gross receipts exceeding Rs 50 lakhs. Also applicable when presumptive taxation under Section 44AD/44ADA is opted but income is below the prescribed percentage.

What is the due date for tax audit report?

30 September of the assessment year (e.g., 30 September 2026 for FY 2025-26). If transfer pricing provisions apply, the due date is 31 October. Late filing of tax audit report attracts penalty of 0.5% of turnover or Rs 1.5 lakhs, whichever is lower, under Section 271B.

Can the same CA do both statutory audit and tax audit?

For companies, the statutory auditor typically also does the tax audit using Form 3CA. For non-corporate entities, any practising CA can be appointed as tax auditor. There is no restriction on the same CA performing both audits.
