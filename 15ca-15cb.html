Form 15CA / 15CB Filing

Form 15CA is an online declaration by the remitter (payer) for any foreign remittance, filed on the income tax e-filing portal. Form 15CB is a CA certificate certifying the nature of remittance, applicable tax rate, DTAA benefit (if claimed), and TDS compliance. Together, they ensure that all cross-border payments from India comply with Section 195 of the Income Tax Act. When Is 15CA/15CB Required?

Regulatory basis: Section 195 read with Rule 37BB, Income Tax Rules. Form 15CA filed online on incometax.gov.in. Form 15CB uploaded by the CA.

Required for all foreign remittances that are taxable under the Income Tax Act. Categories:

Part A (15CA only): Remittances not exceeding ₹5 lakhs in a financial year AND not chargeable to tax.

Part B (15CA only): Remittances covered by RBI\' general permission under Section 195(6) — specified list of remittances (imports, freight, etc.).

Part C (15CA + 15CB): All other remittances — requires CA certificate in Form 15CB certifying tax rate, DTAA applicability, and TDS compliance. This is the most common category for service payments, royalties, FTS, and dividend payments to foreign entities.

Part D (15CA + Order): Remittances where an order/certificate under Section 195(2)/195(3)/197 has been obtained.

When Is Form 15CA / 15CB Filing Not Required?

Form 15CA/15CB may not be required when: (a) the remittance falls under exempted categories in Rule 37BB (personal remittances, education fees below Rs 7 lakhs, medical treatment below Rs 7 lakhs), (b) the remittance amount does not exceed Rs 5 lakhs in a financial year (Form 15CA Part A — self-declaration, no CA certificate needed), (c) the remittance is to a foreign government or specified international organisations, or (d) the payment is not chargeable to tax in India. For amounts exceeding Rs 5 lakhs on taxable payments, CA certificate in Form 15CB is mandatory.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 15CB needed for all foreign payments?

No. Only for Part C remittances (taxable payments exceeding ₹5 lakhs not covered by RBI general permission). Imports of goods and certain specified payments require only 15CA Part A or B.

What is the DTAA benefit?

Double Taxation Avoidance Agreements between India and other countries may provide lower tax rates or exemptions for specific types of income (royalties, FTS, dividends, interest). We analyse the applicable DTAA and claim benefits in Form 15CB to reduce withholding tax.

How quickly can you file 15CA/15CB?

Standard: 2-3 working days. Express (urgent payment): same-day for straightforward remittances. Requires: invoice, agreement, PAN of payee (if available), TRC (Tax Residency Certificate) for DTAA claims.

When is Form 15CA/15CB required?

Required for all foreign remittances (except specified exemptions like imports, embassy payments, student fees up to threshold). 15CA is the remittance information form. 15CB is the CA certificate certifying taxability and applicable DTAA.

Who signs Form 15CB?

A practicing Chartered Accountant (not the assessee themselves even if they are a CA). The CA certifies the nature of remittance, applicable TDS rate, DTAA benefit claimed, and tax deposited. It is a professional certification under Section 195(6).

What are the different parts of Form 15CA?

Part A: remittance below ₹5 lakhs (no 15CB needed). Part B: remittance covered under Section 195(2)/195(3)/197 order. Part C: remittance where 15CB is required (above ₹5 lakhs, taxable). Part D: remittance not taxable under IT Act.

What is the penalty for not filing 15CA/15CB?

Bank cannot process the remittance without 15CA acknowledgment. Penalty under Section 271-I: ₹1 lakh for failure to furnish information. Tax demand on remitter if TDS obligation not complied with.

How long does 15CA/15CB processing take?

CA can issue 15CB within 1-2 working days if documents are ready. 15CA filed online on Income Tax portal — instant acknowledgment. Bank processes remittance within 1-2 days of receiving 15CA acknowledgment number.
