TDS Is Government Money Held in Trust — Non-Deposit Is Prosecution Risk

When you deduct TDS from an employee's salary or a vendor's payment, that money belongs to the government from the moment of deduction. Holding it — even for 1 month beyond the due date — triggers 1.5% interest per month, penalty equal to the TDS amount under Section 271C, and prosecution under Section 276B (3 months to 7 years imprisonment). This is not a compliance formality — it is the single most severe penalty provision in the Income Tax Act. For directors: personal liability applies — the officer responsible for the default can be prosecuted. The 26AS Reconciliation — Why It's Non-Negotiable

26AS reconciliation catches two critical issues: (1) As deductor: TDS you deducted but didn't deposit doesn't appear in the deductee's 26AS — meaning your employee/vendor doesn't get the TDS credit they're entitled to. They will complain, and the department will investigate. (2) As deductee: TDS deducted by your clients but not deposited means you don't get the TDS credit in your ITR. You pay more tax than you should. Virtual Auditor performs quarterly reconciliation — catching mismatches within 90 days instead of at ITR filing time.

TDS and Employee Fraud Risk

TDS fraud is a real risk in SMEs — the accountant deducts TDS from vendor payments but either doesn't deposit it (pocketing the difference) or deposits it against a different TAN. This creates prosecution risk for the director (Section 276B) for an act committed by a subordinate. See Employee Fraud Detection and Forensic Accounting Services for investigation methodology. Prevention: independent 26AS reconciliation (not by the person who deposits TDS) and monthly challan verification by the director.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What is the due date for TDS deposit?
7th of the following month. March deductions: April 30. Late deposit: 1.5% interest per month + prosecution risk under 276B.
Q2: What is the TDS rate on contractor payments?
Section 194C: 1% for individual/HUF contractors, 2% for company/firm contractors. Threshold: ₹30,000 single payment or ₹1,00,000 aggregate.
Q3: What is Section 194Q?
TDS at 0.1% on purchase of goods exceeding ₹50 lakh, applicable if buyer's turnover exceeds ₹10 crore. Frequently overlooked by businesses.
Q4: Is TDS deducted on GST amount?
No — TDS is deducted on the value excluding GST, provided the invoice separately shows the GST component. If GST is not separately shown, TDS is on the full invoice amount.
Q5: What is 26AS reconciliation?
Quarterly verification that TDS deducted by you appears in deductees' 26AS, and TDS deducted on you appears in your 26AS. Catches non-deposits and credit mismatches.
Q6: Can directors be prosecuted for TDS defaults?
Yes — Section 276B: imprisonment 3 months to 7 years for failure to deposit TDS after deduction. The director/officer responsible is personally liable.
Q7: What is a lower TDS certificate?
Section 197: apply Form 13 if actual tax liability will be lower than TDS being deducted. AO issues certificate specifying lower/nil rate. Useful for exporters, loss-making companies.
