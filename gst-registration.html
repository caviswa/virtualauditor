What is GST registration? Goods and Services Tax registration is mandatory for businesses exceeding threshold turnover (₹40 lakhs for goods, ₹20 lakhs for services, ₹10 lakhs for special category states) under Section 22 of the CGST Act, 2017. Registration provides a 15-digit GSTIN required for collecting tax, claiming Input Tax Credit, and filing returns. When Is GST Registration Mandatory?

Regulatory basis: Sections 22-24, CGST Act 2017. Registration within 30 days of becoming liable. Voluntary registration available under Section 25(3) regardless of turnover.

Mandatory registration (regardless of turnover): (a) E-commerce sellers (Section 24(ix)), (b) persons making inter-state taxable supply, (c) casual taxable persons, (d) non-resident taxable persons, (e) persons liable to pay tax under reverse charge, (f) agents of registered suppliers, (g) ISD, and (h) persons deducting TDS under GST.

Threshold-based registration: Aggregate turnover exceeding ₹40 lakhs (goods) or ₹20 lakhs (services) in a financial year. ₹10 lakhs for special category states (Manipur, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura).

Documents Required

PAN of business entity, Aadhaar of proprietor/partners/directors, photograph, address proof of business premises (rent agreement + NOC or ownership document), bank statement or cancelled cheque, electricity bill of premises, and constitution documents (partnership deed, MOA/AOA for companies, LLP agreement).

GST Registration for E-Commerce Sellers

E-commerce sellers face unique GST requirements under Section 24(ix):

Mandatory registration: Every person supplying through an e-commerce operator must register, regardless of turnover. The threshold exemption does not apply.

TCS compliance: E-commerce operators (Amazon, Flipkart, Meesho) deduct TCS at 1% of net taxable value. Sellers must claim this as credit in their returns.

Multi-state complications: If you sell across states through e-commerce, you may need registration in each state where you have a physical presence (warehouse, fulfilment centre). Even FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon) creates presence in states where Amazon warehouses hold your inventory.

We advise on: single vs. multi-state registration strategy, TCS reconciliation, input credit optimisation for e-commerce sellers, and compliance structuring to minimise multi-state burden.

Composition Scheme — Simplified Compliance

The Composition Scheme under Section 10 offers simplified compliance for small taxpayers:

Eligibility: Turnover up to ₹1.5 crore (goods manufacturers/traders) or ₹50 lakhs (service providers). Not available for: inter-state suppliers, e-commerce sellers, ice cream/tobacco/pan masala manufacturers, and casual/non-resident taxable persons.

Tax rates: Manufacturers: 1% of turnover. Traders: 1%. Restaurants: 5%. Service providers: 6%. These are flat rates — no input tax credit available.

Compliance: Quarterly return (GSTR-4) instead of monthly. Annual return (GSTR-4 Annual). No e-invoicing requirement. No e-way bill generation (but must carry e-way bill if received by a regular dealer).

Trade-off: Simpler compliance but no ITC means higher effective tax cost. Suitable for businesses with low input costs (services, retail) where ITC benefit is minimal. Not suitable for businesses with high input costs (manufacturing with raw material purchases) where ITC loss outweighs compliance savings.

We advise on: composition vs. regular registration economics, threshold monitoring (mandatory conversion to regular if turnover crosses ₹1.5 crore), and transition filing requirements.

GST Registration for Non-Residents and Foreigners

Non-resident taxable persons and foreigners can register under GST (Section 24(v)) with specific requirements:

Application: Filed at least 5 days before commencing business in India. Registration valid for the period specified (minimum 1 day, maximum 90 days, extendable).

Advance tax deposit: Equal to the estimated tax liability for the registration period. Deposited at the time of application.

Authorised signatory: Must appoint an authorised person in India as a signatory for GST filings.

GSTIN format: Same 15-digit structure. First 2 digits: state code of the state where the non-resident intends to supply.

Practical application: Foreign companies exhibiting at Indian trade shows, executing projects in India, or providing services from a temporary establishment. Also applicable to foreign e-commerce operators serving Indian customers.

State-Wise GST Registration Nuances

While GST is a national tax, the registration process has state-specific nuances that cause confusion and rejection:

Tamil Nadu: Strict address proof requirements — must be a commercial premises or residential with specific commercial usage permission from the municipality. Aadhaar authentication may face OTP delays in certain districts.

Karnataka: Bangalore-specific: many co-working space addresses face additional scrutiny. The officer may schedule a physical verification visit. We advise on address documentation that satisfies Karnataka GST officers.

Maharashtra: Mumbai registrations face high volume — processing times may be longer (7-10 days vs. 3-5 in other states). Additional documentation for Shops and Establishments Act number may be requested.

Multi-state registration: Businesses operating in multiple states need separate GSTIN per state. Each registration requires a state-specific address proof and authorised signatory. We handle multi-state registrations as a package, ensuring consistent classification (HSN/SAC codes) across all states to avoid reconciliation issues in GST returns.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does GST registration take?

3-7 working days from application submission, assuming documents are in order and Aadhaar authentication is successful. Rejection or query from the officer may extend the timeline.

Is GST registration mandatory for e-commerce sellers?

Yes. Under Section 24(ix), every person supplying goods/services through an e-commerce operator must register regardless of turnover threshold.

Can a foreigner get GST registration in India?

Yes. Non-resident taxable persons register under Section 24(v). Registration valid for the period specified (minimum 1 day). Advance tax deposit equal to estimated liability required.

What is the composition scheme?

Section 10 allows small taxpayers (turnover up to ₹1.5 crore for goods, ₹50 lakhs for services) to pay tax at a flat rate (1-6%) without ITC. Simpler compliance — quarterly returns.

Why register through a CA firm instead of doing it myself?

Registration is day one. What follows is monthly return filing, ITC reconciliation, annual return, and potential notices/appeals. A CA firm provides the complete lifecycle — registration, compliance, and defence. We are also positioned to handle GST appeals if any dispute arises.

What is the Input Service Distributor (ISD) registration?

ISD registration under Section 24(viii) is required when a head office or corporate office receives common invoices for services (rent, professional fees, software licences) consumed by multiple branches in different states. The ISD distributes the input tax credit to respective branch GSTINs proportionally. Mandatory if your business has centralised procurement with multi-state operations.

Can GST registration be cancelled voluntarily?

Yes. Apply for cancellation via Form GST REG-16 on the GST portal if: business is discontinued, transferred, or turnover falls below threshold. Final return (GSTR-10) must be filed within 3 months of cancellation. We handle the cancellation process including ITC reversal computation and final return filing.
