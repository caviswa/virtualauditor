Frequently Asked Questions

What is the tax benefit under Startup India?

Section 80-IAC: 100% deduction of profits for 3 consecutive years out of the first 10 years from incorporation. Available only to companies (not LLPs) with turnover under ₹100 crore. Requires inter-ministerial board certificate.

Can LLPs get DPIIT recognition?

Yes. LLPs can get DPIIT recognition. However, the Section 80-IAC tax exemption applies only to companies, not LLPs.

How long does DPIIT registration take?

Online application via startupindia.gov.in. DPIIT recognition: typically 2-5 working days. Section 80-IAC certification: additional process through inter-ministerial board, 30-60 days.

What qualifies as a startup under DPIIT?

Entity incorporated/registered in India for less than 10 years. Turnover less than ₹100 crore in any financial year. Working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/processes/services. Not formed by splitting or reconstruction.

What benefits does DPIIT recognition provide?

Self-certification for labour and environment laws (3 years). Tax exemption under Section 80-IAC (3 of 10 years). Fast-track patent examination (80% rebate on patent filing). Easy winding up under IBC (90 days). Fund of Funds eligibility.

How to apply for DPIIT startup recognition?

Register on startupindia.gov.in. Provide: incorporation certificate, brief description of innovation, recommendation letter or patent/support. Approval: 2-3 working days for most applications.

Is DPIIT recognition needed for fundraising?

Not mandatory for fundraising. But provides: angel tax exemption (for old cases), labour law self-certification, patent fast-track, and credibility with investors. Recommended for all startups.

Can an LLP get DPIIT startup recognition?

Yes. Both Pvt Ltd and LLP can get DPIIT recognition. However, LLPs cannot issue equity shares, so fundraising advantages are limited to Pvt Ltd. Partnership firms can also apply.
