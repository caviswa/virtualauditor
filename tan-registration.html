TAN Registration — Who Needs It and When

Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (TAN) is a mandatory 10-digit alphanumeric number required under Section 203A of the Income Tax Act, 1961 for every person responsible for deducting or collecting tax at source.

When TDS/TCS Obligation Arises

SectionPayment TypeTDS RateThreshold
192SalaryAs per slabBasic exemption
194AInterest (non-bank)10%₹40,000/year

194HCommission5%₹15,000/year

Application process: Online through NSDL portal using Form 49B. Processing fee: ₹65 + GST. TAN issued within 7-15 working days. Must quote TAN on every TDS challan, TDS certificate (Form 16/16A), and TDS return.

Penalty for non-compliance: Section 272BB: ₹10,000 for failure to apply for or quote TAN. All TDS deposits without TAN are treated as non-compliant — potential disallowance of expenditure under Section 40(a)(ia).

Source: Income Tax Act, 1961 — Section 203A. Income Tax Portal ↗
