Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)

What is a DSC? A Digital Signature Certificate is the electronic equivalent of a physical signature, used to sign documents filed electronically with government authorities (MCA, Income Tax, GST, DGFT). Issued by Certifying Authorities licensed under the Information Technology Act, 2000. Required for: company directors (MCA filings), income tax e-verification, GST registration, e-tendering, and DGFT (import/export). Virtual Auditor provides same-day DSC issuance with Aadhaar e-KYC. When Is Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) Not Required?

A DSC may not be required when: (a) all company directors are based outside India and have alternative signing arrangements accepted by MCA, (b) the entity is a sole proprietorship with no company law filings, (c) an existing valid DSC covers all required portal registrations, or (d) the signing authority has been delegated to a professional (CA/CS) who holds their own DSC. DSC is mandatory for all company directors, authorised signatories for GST returns, and professionals filing statutory returns.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I get DSC?

Same-day with Aadhaar e-KYC. Without Aadhaar: 1-2 days with video verification.

Class 2 vs Class 3?

Class 2: for MCA/ROC filings, income tax, GST. Class 3: for e-tendering, e-procurement, high-security applications.

What is the validity?

1 or 2 years. Must be renewed before expiry for uninterrupted filing capability.

What is a Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)?

Electronic equivalent of a physical signature. Issued by Certifying Authorities (CAs) licensed by CCA India. Used for MCA filings, IT returns, GST registration, e-tendering, and EPFO. Legally valid under IT Act, 2000.

What is the difference between Class 2 and Class 3 DSC?

Class 2: used for MCA, Income Tax, GST, EPFO filings. Sufficient for most business purposes. Class 3: required for e-tendering (government procurement), high-security applications. Class 3 requires in-person verification.

How long does DSC take and what does it cost?

Processing: 1-3 working days. Cost: ₹800-₹1,500 for Class 2 (2-year validity). ₹2,000-₹3,500 for Class 3. Renewal: before expiry, same process. Required documents: PAN, Aadhaar, photo, email, mobile number.

Can one person have multiple DSCs?

Yes. You can have separate DSCs for different purposes (one for signing, one for encryption). Directors of multiple companies use same DSC across all companies. Each DSC is linked to PAN and Aadhaar.

What happens if DSC expires?

Cannot file any electronic forms (MCA, IT, GST) until renewed. Renewal must be done before expiry for uninterrupted service. Old DSC cannot be renewed — a new DSC is issued. Old signed documents remain valid.
