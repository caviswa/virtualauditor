Import Export Code (IEC) — Complete Guide

IEC is a 10-digit unique business identification number issued by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) under the Foreign Trade Policy. It is mandatory for any person or entity importing or exporting goods from/to India.

Who Needs IEC

ActivityIEC Required?Notes
Import of goodsYes (mandatory)Customs won't clear without IEC
Export of goodsYes (mandatory)Shipping bill requires IEC
Export of servicesRecommendedBanks may ask for receipts
Import for personal useNoBelow ₹25,000 value
Government departmentsExemptNotified entities

Application process: Online on DGFT portal (dgft.gov.in). Form ANF 2A with PAN, Aadhaar, bank account details, and address proof. Fee: ₹500. Processing: 3-5 working days. Lifetime validity — no renewal needed.

Post-IEC compliance: Register on ICEGATE (Indian Customs Electronic Gateway) for customs clearance. Obtain Authorised Dealer Code from your bank. For exporters: apply for GST LUT (zero-rated exports without IGST). Consider RCMC (Registration Cum Membership Certificate) from Export Promotion Council for incentives.

Source: Foreign Trade (Development & Regulation) Act, 1992. Foreign Trade Policy 2023. DGFT Portal ↗
