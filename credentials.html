IBBI Registered Valuer — Securities & Financial Assets

Regulatory basis: Registration: IBBI/RV/03/2019/12333. Asset Class: Securities or Financial Assets. Registered under IBBI (Registered Valuers) Regulations, 2017, read with Section 247 of the Companies Act, 2013.

What this enables: Issuance of valuation reports that are legally mandated under: Companies Act (Section 247 — any valuation required under the Act), IBC (Regulation 35 — fair value and liquidation value for CIRP), and accepted by RBI (FEMA pricing), SEBI (listed company valuations), and Income Tax authorities (Rule 11UA).

Verification: Search at ibbi.gov.in under Registered Valuers → Securities or Financial Assets.

Fellow Chartered Accountant (FCA) — ICAI

Issuing body: The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI), established under the Chartered Accountants Act, 1949.

Credential level: Fellow (FCA) — highest level, requiring 5+ years of continuous membership, 150 hours of Continuing Professional Education, and active Certificate of Practice.

What this enables: Statutory audit, tax audit, FEMA certification, income tax representation, GST appellate representation, transfer pricing documentation, and all services reserved for Chartered Accountants under Indian law.

Verification: icai.org → Member Search.

Associate Company Secretary (ACS) — ICSI

Issuing body: The Institute of Company Secretaries of India (ICSI), established under the Company Secretaries Act, 1980.

What this enables: Company secretarial services under the Companies Act, 2013 — board resolution drafting, statutory register maintenance, ROC filings, corporate governance advisory, secretarial audit (for certain classes of companies), and appearance before NCLT.

Verification: icsi.edu → Member Search.

Certified Fraud Examiner (CFE) — ACFE USA

Issuing body: Association of Certified Fraud Examiners (ACFE), Austin, Texas, USA. The world's largest anti-fraud professional organisation with 90,000+ members across 180+ countries.

What this enables: Forensic accounting investigations, fraud examination, financial due diligence, whistleblower investigation support, litigation support and expert witness testimony, asset tracing, and the emerging field of AI model auditing using CFE forensic methodology.

Verification: acfe.com → Verify a CFE.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do credentials matter for valuation services?

Under Indian law, only an IBBI Registered Valuer can issue valuation reports required under the Companies Act (Section 247) and IBC (Regulation 35). A generic CA certificate is not sufficient. Additionally, FEMA valuations require certification by a CA in practice, and SEBI-regulated valuations require independence from the transacting parties. Credentials determine legal validity of the work product.

Can I independently verify these credentials?

Yes. Every credential listed has a public verification mechanism: IBBI registration at ibbi.gov.in, ICAI membership at icai.org, ICSI membership at icsi.edu, and ACFE certification at acfe.com. Registration numbers are provided for verification.

Is the CFE credential recognised in India?

The CFE is globally recognised but not mandated by any Indian regulatory framework. However, the ACFE methodology for fraud examination is the international standard used by law enforcement, regulatory agencies, and forensic practitioners worldwide. In India, CFE-qualified professionals are engaged by courts, SFIO, and corporate boards for fraud investigations.

What does FCA credential mean?

Fellow of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI). FCA is awarded after 5+ years of practice as ACA (Associate CA). Demonstrates extensive experience in audit, tax, and financial advisory.

What is ACS credential?

Associate of the Institute of Company Secretaries of India (ICSI). Qualifies for corporate governance, board compliance, ROC filings, and statutory secretarial work. Essential for Companies Act expertise.

What is CFE credential?

Certified Fraud Examiner from the Association of Certified Fraud Examiners (ACFE), USA. The global standard for fraud examination. Qualifies for forensic investigations, due diligence, and anti-fraud advisory.

What is IBBI Registered Valuer?

Why do multiple credentials matter?

Each credential covers a different regulatory domain. FCA: financial analysis. ACS: corporate governance. CFE: fraud detection. IBBI RV: statutory valuation. The intersection enables multi-regulatory practice that single-credential firms cannot offer.
